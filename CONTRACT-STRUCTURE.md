# ClauseKit — Contract Structure & Mandatory Clause Spec
**Version 1.0 | 26 March 2026**

---

## Overview

Every contract generated by ClauseKit must follow a consistent, professional structure. This document defines:

1. The mandatory elements every contract must contain
2. The section structure and order
3. What each section must include
4. The AI prompt standard that enforces this

The reference document is the JWebDesign Ltd / Primrose Ever Care Web Design Agreement — that is the quality bar every generated contract should meet.

---

## The Non-Negotiables

These elements must appear in **every** contract regardless of type:

| Element | Why |
|---------|-----|
| Document header with title and date | Identifies the document |
| Party information block | Names both parties, their roles, addresses, emails |
| Agreement title clause | States who is agreeing to what |
| Governing law clause | UK legal requirement — specifies English & Welsh law |
| GDPR / data processing clause | UK legal requirement — data shared between parties |
| Late payment clause | References Late Payment of Commercial Debts Act 1998 |
| Confidentiality clause | Protects both parties |
| Termination clause | Exit rights and consequences |
| IP / ownership clause | Who owns what and when |
| Signature block | Both parties, signature line, printed name, date |
| Footer disclaimer | "AI-generated. Not legal advice." |

## Universal Document Structure

Every contract uses this section order. Sections marked **[MANDATORY]** appear in every contract. Sections marked **[TYPE-SPECIFIC]** only appear when relevant.

```
HEADER
  - Document title (e.g. "Freelance Project Agreement")
  - Agreement date
  - Both party names visible

PARTY INFORMATION BLOCK [MANDATORY]
  - Party 1: Full name / company, address, email
  - Party 2: Full name / company, address, email

01. AGREEMENT TITLE [MANDATORY]
    Who is entering into this agreement, in what capacity, under what terms.

02. PROJECT / SCOPE OVERVIEW [TYPE-SPECIFIC]
    What is being done. Plain English description of the work or relationship.

03. SCOPE OF WORK / SERVICES [TYPE-SPECIFIC]
    Bulleted list of exactly what is included. And what is NOT included.

04. DELIVERABLES [TYPE-SPECIFIC]
    What the client/other party receives on completion.

05. TIMELINES [TYPE-SPECIFIC]
    Start date, end date, milestones, dependencies (e.g. client content).

06. FEES & PAYMENT TERMS [MANDATORY where payment exists]
    Amount, structure (fixed / hourly / milestone), payment schedule.
    Late Payment Act reference — 8% above BoE base rate on overdue invoices.
    Payment method.

07. REVISION POLICY [TYPE-SPECIFIC — Freelance, Client Service, Retainer]
    Number of rounds included, what counts as a revision, cost of extras.

08. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY [MANDATORY — all except NDA, Late Payment]
    When IP transfers. Who owns what until then. Portfolio rights.
    Third-party assets clause.

09. IR35 / TAX STATUS [MANDATORY — Freelance, Retainer, Subcontractor]
    Self-employed status confirmation. Tax obligations remain with contractor.

10. CONFIDENTIALITY [MANDATORY]
    Both parties protect confidential information.
    Duration. Standard exceptions (public domain, prior knowledge, legal compulsion).

11. DATA PROTECTION [MANDATORY]
    UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018 acknowledgement.
    What personal data is shared, for what purpose.

12. TERMINATION [MANDATORY]
    Notice period. Who can terminate. What happens to work in progress.
    What happens to fees already paid.

13. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY [TYPE-SPECIFIC — Client Service, Retainer, Subcontractor]
    Cap on liability. Exclusion of consequential losses.

14. GOVERNING LAW & JURISDICTION [MANDATORY]
    English and Welsh law. Courts of England and Wales have jurisdiction.

ACCEPTANCE & SIGNATURES [MANDATORY]
  - Acceptance text ("By signing, both parties confirm...")
  - Party 1 signature line, printed name, date
  - Party 2 signature line, printed name, date

FOOTER [MANDATORY]
  - "AI-generated document. Not legal advice."
  - ClauseKit branding
```

## Section Detail — Mandatory Clauses

### Agreement Title (Section 01)
Every contract must open with a clear statement of:
- Who Party 1 is (full name, trading name, address)
- Who Party 2 is (full name, trading name, address)
- What this agreement governs ("...governs the provision of [service] as described herein")
- That it becomes binding on signature

**Example:**
> This Freelance Project Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on [DATE] between **[YOUR NAME], [YOUR COMPANY]**, of [YOUR ADDRESS] ("Contractor") and **[CLIENT NAME], [CLIENT COMPANY]**, of [CLIENT ADDRESS] ("Client"). This Agreement governs the provision of [DESCRIPTION OF WORK] and becomes legally binding upon signature of both parties.

---

### Fees & Payment Terms
Must include:
- Total fee or rate
- Payment structure (single payment / split / milestones / monthly)
- Invoice payment terms (7 / 14 / 30 days)
- Late payment: *"Invoices unpaid after [X] days of the due date will accrue interest at 8% above the Bank of England base rate per annum under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998."*
- Fixed compensation per invoice: £40 for invoices under £1,000; £70 for £1,000–£10,000; £100 over £10,000
- Payment method

---

### Intellectual Property
Must include:
- IP belongs to contractor until **full payment received**
- On full payment: ownership transfers to client
- Contractor retains right to display work in portfolio (unless client requests otherwise in writing)
- Third-party assets remain under their respective licences

---

### Confidentiality
Must include:
- Both parties keep confidential info private during and after the agreement
- Standard exceptions: (a) publicly available information; (b) information independently developed; (c) disclosure required by law or court order
- Survives termination of the agreement

---

### Data Protection
Must include:
- Reference to UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018
- What personal data is shared (party names, addresses, emails at minimum)
- Data is used only for the purpose of this agreement
- Neither party will share the other's personal data with third parties without consent

---

### Termination
Must include:
- Notice period (e.g. 7 / 14 / 30 days written notice)
- On Client termination: deposit non-refundable; work to date invoiced
- On Contractor termination: unused fees refunded less work done to date
- All outstanding invoices due immediately on termination

---

### Governing Law
Must include:
> This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

## Per-Contract Type — Additional Mandatory Sections

### Freelance / Project Agreement
Additional mandatory: Project Overview, Scope of Work, Deliverables, Timelines, Revision Policy, IR35 clause

### NDA (Mutual & One-Way)
Drop: IP, IR35, Fees, Revision Policy, Timelines, Deliverables
Add: Definition of Confidential Information, Permitted Use, Duration, Remedies for Breach

### Retainer Agreement
Additional mandatory: Services Description, Monthly Hours, Minimum Term, Auto-renewal, IR35 clause

### Subcontractor Agreement
Additional mandatory: Sub-contracted Work Description, Quality Warranty, IP flows to main contractor, IR35 clause

### Client Service Agreement
Additional mandatory: Services Description, Liability Cap, Exclusion of Consequential Loss, Dispute Resolution

### Website T&Cs
Replace party agreement structure with: Website owner + user relationship, Acceptable Use, GDPR Privacy Policy reference, Cookie Policy reference, Limitation of Liability, Right to Amend

### Late Payment Letter
Replace agreement structure with: Demand letter format. Invoice details, statutory interest calculation, fixed compensation, final deadline, legal action warning

### Employment Offer Letter
Replace agreement structure with: Offer letter format. Role, salary, start date, holiday, probation, notice, employment particulars note (ERA 1996 s.1)

## AI Prompt Standard

The system prompt sent to GPT-4o must:

1. **Specify exact section numbering** — tell the AI to use the format: `01. SECTION TITLE`
2. **Name all mandatory sections** for the contract type
3. **Provide party names and details** explicitly in the user prompt
4. **Specify formatting requirements** — clear headings, indented body text, bullet lists for deliverables/scope
5. **Enforce mandatory clauses** — list every clause that must appear regardless of what user said
6. **Set the tone** — formal, plain-English, not overly legalistic

### System Prompt Structure

```
[ROLE]
You are a specialist UK legal document drafter producing bespoke contracts for 
UK freelancers and small businesses.

[MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS — ALWAYS INCLUDE]
Every contract you produce MUST include:
- Clear numbered sections in format: 01. SECTION TITLE
- A party information block naming both parties, their roles, addresses and emails
- Governing law: English and Welsh law. Courts of England and Wales have jurisdiction.
- Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 reference where payment terms exist
- UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 data processing acknowledgement
- Confidentiality clause with standard exceptions
- Termination clause with notice period and consequences
- Signature block: Party 1 and Party 2, each with signature line, printed name, date
- Footer: "This document was AI-generated by ClauseKit. It is not legal advice."

[TYPE-SPECIFIC SECTIONS]
For this contract type, also include: [list per type]

[FORMATTING]
- Use numbered sections: 01. TITLE, 02. TITLE, etc.
- Section body: plain prose paragraphs and/or bullet lists
- Fees: present in a structured breakdown (item | amount format)
- Payment split: describe each instalment clearly
- No markdown code fences or asterisk formatting
- Output as clean formatted plain text only
- Approximate length: 800–1,500 words depending on complexity

[PROFESSIONAL STANDARD]
- Every clause must be complete and specific
- Include actual values from the user's data (names, fees, dates)
- Use plain English — no unnecessary legal jargon
- Be specific — "7 days written notice" not "reasonable notice"
```
